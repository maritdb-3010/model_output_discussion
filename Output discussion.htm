Marit de Beijer – Modeling for Sustainability

Figure 1 Water uptake in a river per timestep per player without implementation of diplomacy

The output I chose represents the water uptake in a water course per 150 timesteps of the three players (from upstream to downstream). Additionally, this output excludes the implementation of diplomacy. There are a lot of peaks and drops in the graph, which suggests that uptake differs over time and depends on the moods of the players. However, the water uptake is not very realistic, as the watercourse in this case is static. The water flow simply doesn’t fluctuate over time. There are certain aspects that can be taken into account to solve this problem. Firstly, seasonal changes can be implemented to make the system more dynamic. Other implementation, apart from implementations regarding to the water flow, that can be inserted is pollution. Pollution is done by agents, which can result in a difference in water availability. When an agent pollutes in large sizes, this can limit the water availability.
